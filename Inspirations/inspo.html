My Inspirations

So on this page I've decided to compile a short list of a few of my favorite modern Photographers. These are people I look up to and gain inspiration from.

Jennifer Bin

Jennifer Bin is an Toronto born UX/UI designer and Photographer currently working in Shangai. Bin takes urban photographs, mostly from high up on rooftops.
Her photographs provide an incredible hard to get view of the world.
It's unique in the sense that many of us won't ever get the chance to experience the views that she's had to capture her amazing images.
Luckily we can view them from the safety of our homes on our computers and phones.


It’s easy to think that a photo with many likes on Instagram is a good photo, but I always try to work on my own barometer of what makes a good photo, instead of letting the crowd decide for me.

Andre Josselin

Andre Josselin is German born traveling photographer. His photos give off a very filmic look. A unique style that shines on its own.
Almost all of his photos are shot with natrual light. He successfully captures the mood of the moment.


Nothing should look like it is staged, and overly retouched. And that’s how it works. It’s just me, the camera, the subject, the situation, and the landscapes.

Josh Tep

Josh Tep is in my opinion, the king of lowlight photography. Tep was born and raised in Cleveland, Ohio.
His dark style is very distinctive as its not one that you see often when it comes to photography
When I look at his photos, I also think cinematography. Many of his photos look like they're a single frame from a futuristic movie.
